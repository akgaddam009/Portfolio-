# 🧠 UX Case Study Writer (Claude)

## 🎯 Goal
Write a clear, concise UX case study that a non-domain hiring manager can understand in <30 seconds.

---

## 🧾 Input (fill before running)
- Project Name:
- Domain (e.g. Fintech, SaaS, Healthcare):
- Users:
- Problem (raw):
- What changed (solution summary):
- Impact (metrics or outcomes):
- Your role:
- Constraints:

---

## ✍️ Writing Instructions

You are a **UX writer + product storyteller**.

Rewrite this case study to:
- Be understood by **non-domain hiring managers**
- Focus on **clarity > jargon**
- Highlight **business + user impact**
- Remove unnecessary process detail
- Keep it **tight, scannable, and outcome-driven**

---

## 🧩 Output Structure

### 1. 🪄 Hook (2 lines max)
- What changed + why it matters

### 2. 🧠 Context (simple, non-jargon)
- What the product/company does
- Who the users are
- Why this problem matters

### 3. ⚠️ Problem (reframed)
- What was broken (in plain English)
- Real-world analogy if possible

### 4. 💡 Solution (clear, not feature dump)
- What you changed
- Key decisions (only important ones)

### 5. 📈 Impact (must be sharp)
- Time saved / errors reduced / clarity improved
- Before → After

### 6. 🧑‍💻 Your Role
- What you owned vs collaborated

---

## 🧪 Writing Style Rules
- Use **short sentences**
- Avoid domain jargon (rewrite finance/tech terms)
- Prefer **before → after framing**
- No fluff like "we leveraged" or "seamless experience"
- Make it sound like explaining to a smart outsider

---

## 🔁 Optional Enhancements
- Add a **one-line analogy** (e.g. "like switching from Excel chaos to a GPS")
- Add **numbers wherever possible**
- Keep total length under **250–300 words**

---

## 🚫 Avoid
- Long design process explanation
- Tool mentions (Figma, Miro etc.)
- Generic UX phrases
