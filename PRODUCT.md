# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, design leads, and recruiters at product-led tech companies evaluating Arun for senior IC or lead designer roles. Secondary: potential consulting clients and collaborators. They arrive with intent — they've seen a referral, a LinkedIn post, or a case study link — and spend 2–5 minutes deciding whether to reach out. They are design-literate and will notice if the portfolio contradicts the work it's advertising.

## Product Purpose

Arun Gaddam's personal portfolio. Demonstrates senior product design craft across enterprise SaaS, B2B AI tools, and workflow platforms. The portfolio itself is proof of the work — it is built by the designer using the same design thinking and execution standard as the case studies inside it. Success means a recruiter or hiring manager reads the first panel, trusts the signal, and sends a message.

## Brand Personality

Precise, warm, quietly confident. The voice is direct without being cold, considered without being verbose. It practises what it preaches: a designer who claims to care about enterprise UX should have a portfolio that is usable, fast, accessible, and beautiful — not just visually impressive on first scroll.

## Anti-references

- Bloated creative agency sites with full-screen video heroes and 8-second load times
- Over-animated Awwwards showcases where motion is the product
- Generic Framer/Webflow portfolio templates (dark card grid, big emoji, "I make cool stuff")
- Clinical SaaS white — pure-white backgrounds that feel like a hospital, not a designed artefact
- Portfolios that bury the work behind personality — the case studies are the point

## Design Principles

1. **The portfolio is the proof.** Every design decision — spacing, typography, interaction — should demonstrate the same standard of craft the case studies claim. If it's sloppy here, recruiters assume the work is too.
2. **Restraint over decoration.** Every element earns its place. Delight should feel discovered, not performed.
3. **Warm precision.** Linear-inspired rigour (tight typography, consistent tokens, intentional hierarchy) softened by warmth (off-white backgrounds, human copy, the availability dot that pulses like a heartbeat).
4. **Show, don't explain.** The hero copy does not list skills. The work panels demonstrate them. Avoid restating what the work already communicates.
5. **Accessible by default.** WCAG AA minimum. Touch targets ≥ 44px. Reduced motion respected. The designer who builds inaccessible portfolios undermines their own UX credentials.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Dark mode fully supported via CSS custom properties and `data-theme` attribute. Reduced motion respected via `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)`. Touch targets ≥ 44px enforced on all interactive elements. Custom cursor suppressed on touch/pointer-coarse devices.
